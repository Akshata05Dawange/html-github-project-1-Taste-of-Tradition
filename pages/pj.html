Sarson ka Saag & Makki ki Roti

Sarson ka saag and makki ki roti are a quintessential Punjabi winter dish that is a staple of the Lohri festival. This combination of mustard greens and cornmeal flatbread symbolizes the harvest and is a hearty, nutritious meal enjoyed during the cold months, often served with a dollop of butter or ghee and seasonal accompaniments like gur (jaggery).

🌟Ingredients for Sarson ka Saag & Makki ki Roti

For Sarson ka Saag:
  • 2 cups mustard leaves (sarson ke patte)
  • 1 cup spinach (palak)
  • ½ cup bathua leaves (optional)
  • 2 green chilies
  • 1 onion (finely chopped)
  • 1 tomato (finely chopped)
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp maize flour (makki ka atta)
  • 3 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Salt to taste
  • Red chili powder & garam masala – to taste
For Makki ki Roti:
  • Maize flour – 2 cups
    • Warm water – as needed
  • Ghee – for cooking
  • Salt – to taste

👩‍🍳 Recipe for Sarson ka Saag & Makki ki Roti

for Sarson ka Saag
  1. Boil greens – Wash and chop all leaves. Boil them with green chilies and a little water for about 10–15 minutes.
  2. Grind the mixture – Let it cool, then grind coarsely to make a thick paste.
  3. Prepare the tadka – Heat ghee in a pan, sauté onion till golden, then add ginger-garlic paste and tomatoes.
  4. Add spices & saag – Add the boiled saag paste, salt, chili powder, and garam masala.
  5. Thicken it – Mix maize flour in a little water, add it to the saag, and cook on low flame for 10–15 minutes.
  6. Serve hot – Top with a spoon of ghee before serving.
for Makki ki Rot
  1. Knead dough – Mix maize flour with warm water and salt to form a soft dough.
  2. Shape the roti – Take a portion, flatten it using your palms or roll it gently between plastic sheets.
  3. Cook – Roast on a hot tawa until golden on both sides, applying ghee or butter.
  4. Serve – Serve hot with Sarson ka Saag, white butter, and jaggery.

Cole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a traditional Punjabi dish, popular for Baisakhi, that consists of spicy chickpea curry (\(<>chole<>\)) served with fluffy, deep-fried bread (\(<>bhature<>\)). The dish is a festive staple, enjoyed by families to celebrate the Baisakhi harvest, the Punjabi New Year, and the founding of the Khalsa. The bhature are made from maida (refined flour) and yogurt, while the chole are made from chickpeas simmered in a fragrant blend of spices like turmeric, cumin, coriander, and garam masala. 

🌟Ingredients for Chole & Bhature

For Chole (Chickpeas Curry):
  • 1 cup chickpeas (chole / kabuli chana) – soaked overnight
  • 1 onion – finely chopped
  • 1 tomato – finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste/li>
  • 1–2 green chilies (optional)
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1½ tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Salt – to taste
For Bhature (Deep-fried Bread):
  • 2 cups maida (all-purpose flour)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (sooji/rava)
  • ½ cup curd (yogurt)
  • ½ tsp baking soda
  • Salt – to taste
  • Oil – for deep frying
  • Water – as needed to knead

👩‍🍳Chole & Bhature Recipe

for Chole
  1. Boil chickpeas – Pressure cook soaked chickpeas until soft (about 4–5 whistles).
  2. Make masala – Heat oil, add cumin seeds, onions, and sauté till golden brown.
  3. Add spices & tomatoes – Mix in ginger-garlic paste, chili powder, turmeric, coriander powder, and chopped tomatoes. Cook till oil separates.
  4. Add chickpeas – Stir in boiled chole and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Finish – Sprinkle garam masala and garnish with coriander leaves.
for Bhature
  1. Make dough – Combine maida, sooji, baking soda, curd, salt, and a little water. Knead into a soft dough.
  2. Rest – Cover and let it rest for 2–3 hours.
  3. Shape & fry – Divide dough into balls, roll into circles, and deep-fry in hot oil until puffed and golden.

kada prasad

Kada Prasad is a holy offering made in Sikh Gurudwaras, prepared with whole wheat flour, ghee, sugar, and water. It is rich, smooth, and aromatic, symbolizing equality and devotion. Served warm after prayers, it holds deep spiritual and cultural importance in Punjab.

🌟Ingredients for Kada Prasad

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour (atta)
  • 1 cup ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 cups water

👩‍🍳Kada Prasad Recipe

  1. Boil sugar water – In a pan, boil 3 cups of water and dissolve sugar in it. Keep it warm
  2. Roast flour – In another heavy-bottomed pan, heat ghee and add wheat flour.
  3. Cook till golden – Stir continuously on low flame until the flour turns golden brown and gives a nutty aroma.
  4. Add sugar water – Carefully pour the hot sugar water into the roasted flour while stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  5. Cook & thicken – Keep stirring until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
  6. Serve hot – Offer as Prasad or enjoy warm with family.
