Basundi

Basundi is a popular sweet dish from Gujarat made by boiling milk until it thickens, then sweetening it with sugar and flavoring it with cardamom, saffron, and nuts. It is creamy, rich, and often served chilled during festivals and special occasions.

🌟Ingredients for Basundi

  • Full-fat milk – 1 litre (main base)
  • Sugar – ½ cup (adjust to taste)
  • Cardamom powder (elaichi) – ½ teaspoon
  • Saffron strands (kesar) – few strands (for color & flavor)
  • Chopped nuts – almonds, pistachios, and cashews
  • Nutmeg powder (optional) – a pinch
  • Rose water (optional) – 1 teaspoon for aroma

👩‍🍳Basundi Recipe

  1. Boil the milk in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan.
  2. Once it starts boiling, reduce the flame and simmer it.
  3. Stir continuously and scrape the cream (malai) from the sides into the milk.
  4. Continue to boil until the milk reduces to about half its volume and thickens.
  5. Add sugar, cardamom powder, saffron, and chopped nuts. Mix well.
  6. Boil for 5–10 more minutes, then turn off the gas.
  7. Let it cool, add rose water (optional), and refrigerate for a few hours.
  8. Serve chilled Basundi, garnished with nuts and saffron. 😋

Shrikhand

Shrikhand is a traditional Gujarati dessert made from thick strained yogurt (curd), sweetened with sugar and flavored with saffron and cardamom. It is creamy, rich, and often garnished with dry fruits. Served chilled, it is especially popular during festivals and summer.

🌟Ingredients for Shrikhand

  • Thick curd (yogurt) – 2 cups
  • Powdered sugar – ½ cup (adjust to taste)
  • Saffron strands (kesar) – a few strands soaked in 1 tbsp warm milk
  • Cardamom powder (elaichi) – ½ teaspoon
  • Chopped nuts – almonds and pistachios for garnish
  • Rose water – 1 teaspoon (optional)

👩‍🍳Shrikhand Recipe

  1. Strain the curd — Tie the curd in a clean cloth and hang it for 4–5 hours to remove excess water. You’ll get thick hung curd (called chakka).
  2. Transfer to a bowl — Whisk the hung curd until smooth and creamy.
  3. Add flavorings — Mix in powdered sugar, saffron milk, cardamom powder, and rose water.
  4. Blend well — Stir or whisk until the mixture is smooth and slightly thick.
  5. Chill and serve — Refrigerate for 1–2 hours. Serve cold, garnished with chopped nuts and saffron strands.

Lapsi

Lapsi is a traditional Gujarati sweet made from broken wheat (daliya), ghee, and jaggery or sugar. It has a soft, grainy texture and a rich aroma. Lapsi is often prepared during festivals, pujas, and special occasions as a symbol of good luck and prosperity.

🌟Ingredients for Lapsi

  • Broken wheat (daliya/fada) – 1 cup
  • Ghee (clarified butter) – 3 to 4 tablespoons
  • Jaggery (gur) – ¾ cup (or sugar as per taste)
  • Water – 2½ cups
  • Cardamom powder (elaichi) – ½ teaspoon
  • Chopped nuts – almonds, cashews, or raisins (optional)

👩‍🍳Lapsi Recipe

  1. Roast the wheat – Heat ghee in a pan, add the broken wheat, and roast on low flame till golden brown and aromatic.
  2. Boil water – In another pan, boil 2½ cups of water.
  3. Cook the wheat – Add the roasted wheat to the boiling water. Cover and cook on low flame until soft and water is absorbed.
  4. Add sweetness – Mix in grated jaggery (or sugar) and stir until it melts completely
  5. Flavor and garnish – Add cardamom powder and mix well. Garnish with chopped nuts.
  6. Serve hot or warm – Lapsi tastes delicious when served warm with a spoon of ghee on top!
