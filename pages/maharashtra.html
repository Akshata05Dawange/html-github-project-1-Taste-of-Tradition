Puranpoli

Puran Poli is a traditional Maharashtrian sweet flatbread made with a filling of cooked chana dal, jaggery, and cardamom, stuffed inside soft wheat dough. It is golden, soft, and rich in flavor, usually prepared during festivals like Holi and Ganesh Chaturthi.

🌟Ingredients for Puranpoli

  • 1 cup chana dal
  • 1 cup grated jaggery
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 2 cups wheat flour
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • Water as needed

👩‍🍳Puranpoli Recipe

  1. Boil and mash chana dal.
  2. Cook it with jaggery until thick. Add cardamom and cool.
  3. Knead dough from flour, ghee, and water.
  4. Stuff dough with filling, roll gently, and cook both sides with ghee.

Vada Pav

Vada Pav is a popular street food from Maharashtra, often called the “Indian Burger.” It consists of a spicy potato fritter (batata vada) placed inside a soft bread roll (pav), served with chutneys and fried green chilies. It’s a quick, tasty, and affordable snack loved across India.

🌟Ingredients for Vada Pav

  • 4 boiled potatoes
  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 2 green chilies, ginger, curry leaves
  • Salt, turmeric, and oil for frying
  • 6 pav rolls

👩‍🍳Vada Pav Recipe

  1. Make potato mixture with spices and form balls.
  2. Dip them in besan batter and deep fry till golden.
  3. Cut pav, spread chutneys, and place vada inside.

Modak

🌟Ingredients for Modak

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 3/4 cup jaggery
  • 1/2 tsp cardamom powder
  • 1 cup rice flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tsp ghee
  • Pinch of salt

👩‍🍳Modak Recipe

  1. Cook coconut and jaggery until thick.
  2. Boil water with ghee and salt, add rice flour, and mix well.
  3. Knead dough, make small cups, fill with mixture, and shape modaks.
  4. Steam for 10–12 minutes.
